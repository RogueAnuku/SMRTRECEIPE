Smart Receipt Design the Case Study

Conducted between April 12th-April 30th, 2023.

Team: Anika McPhee (UX Researcher), Faye Teodoro (UX Designer), Vaishali Singh (UX Designer), Hill Phatpanichot (UX Designer).

To keep on track of schedule and to maintain a timeline, we utalized the AGILE method and used trello as the tool.

Background:

Our team was challenged to create a mobile app concept that solves a need in users’ everyday lives.

Challenge:

A significant number of people struggle with finding new and exciting recipes that cater to their food preferences. Users also need a way to use the ingredients available to them.

Resolution:

Our mobile application, Home Chef, is crafted to assist individuals with hectic schedules in utilizing ingredients readily available in their cupboards, thereby saving both time and money. Powered by artificial intelligence, the app customizes nutritious recipes according to users' meal preferences. Additionally, it provides alternative ingredients for added flexibility and for maximized benefit.

The Target Demographic:

Young professionals, age ranges betweeen 18yrs-35yrs.

The Method Toolbox:

  • Proto-persona
  • Journey Mapping
  • Interviews
  • Online Survey
    • Affinity Diagram
  • Online Survey
  • Empathy Mapping
  • Competitor Analysis
  • Wireframing
  • User Testing
  • High-Fidelity Prototyping

Findings:

Pain Points

66.7% of users experience pain points related to the length of time healthy meal preparation takes and sited that as a reason meal planning does not occur. 33.3% of users struggled with finding healthy meal options for their unique dietary restrictions.

Based on user research, we've found:

1. Busy schedules make healthy meal planning challenging.

Users prefer quick and easy recipes, avoiding time-consuming videos or complex dishes. Our goal is to create a recipe app that is convenient and time-efficient, with success measured by reduced meal preparation time.

2. People don't know to how to use what's in their pantry thus resulting in a waste of money and food.

Issues we faced...

How and where do we start? How deep do we plan on going into the nutrition/health aspect? How much energy do we put on customization and ingredence based receipe recomendations?

We plan on finding out...

With testing our digital sketch designs on recruited parcticpants and then interperate the data!

Usability study:

We recruited five participants to complete four tasks related to our research questions.

Objectives

We want to access users ability to create a new account with social media. For the purpose of our study, we instructed users to use Google. The reason why is because we've identified time as a pain point for users quick sign-up options are imparitive.

We decided on testing the coaching screens/ on-boarding experience...

"77% of users never use an app again 72 hours after installation."

John Dye Android Authority

With that statistic in mind, we had to nail it! We needed insight on how to proceed. what did users expect ? How Do they respond to our design?

So we divided into pairs. One pair sketched the potential on-boarding screens for the receipes aspect. The other pair created sketches for the health aspect?

What did the participants think of our on-boarding designs?

% of users felt the on-boarding was too long. Did the receipe and health aspect need to be so seperatly detailed? "I wouldn't expect to see this level of health features for a receipe app"- anonymous participant.

Summary:

Our competitor analysis revealed that there is a thriving market for meal planning apps, and our mobile application distinguishes itself by seamlessly integrating various crucial features to enhance user experience. With its unique combination of functionalities, our app caters to individuals managing budgets, navigating tight schedules, and seeking a convenient solution for planning and organizing meals.
